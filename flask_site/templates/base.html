Important Food Related CO2 Ideas To Help Lower Your Carbon Footprint

  • Meat

    Did you know that you can reduce your carbon footprint just by making simple swaps in the foods you eat?

    Increase vegetable portions while decreasing portions of meat. According to many studies, the meat we consume releases more carbon in its production than vegetables! Aim to eat less and more locally sourced meat to reduce the transport offsets! According to a study by the University of Oxford, Vegan diets were found to be the least harmful to the planet in environmental terms (generating the equivalent of almost 3kg of carbon dioxide daily), and meat-heavy the most (over 7kg of CO2 daily). Meat-free diets generate just under 4kg of CO2 a day.

  • Shop Local

    Support local suppliers & reduce your carbon footprint!

    Always choose locally grown produce when possible. This practice reduces the transport distance of your food. Lowering the transport distance directly lowers the carbon emissions of the produce! (Transporting food requires petroleum-based fuels, and many fertilizers are also fossil fuel based.) Plus, by choosing food that is grown locally, it also encourages eating seasonal organic ingredients. A BBC poll has revealed that fewer than one in 10 Brits know when some of the UK’s most well-known fruit and vegetables are in season, and supermarkets do little to help. Check out what the local farmer's market is selling to find out what is in season!

  • The 5R's Rule

    Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, Refuse, Rot

    Follow these 5 simple steps to guarantee a reduction in your carbon footprint. STEP 1: REFUSE. Make smarter purchasing decisions and set standards and expectations by refusing to buy wasteful or non-recyclable products. STEP 2: REDUCE the use of harmful, wasteful, and non-recyclable products. Reducing dependency on these kinds of products results in less waste materials ending up in landfill and the associated negative environmental impacts. Reduce to avoid unnecessary waste! STEP 3: REUSE items throughout your life instead of buying new ones. Start small by carrying a water bottle or reusable coffee cup to cut down on single use plastic and styrofoam. Once you master one area, prioritize reuse for other products in your life like food packaging, cardboard boxes and food containers STEP 4:RECYCLE. Once you've gone through the other steps, recycling is the most environmentally friendly waste disposal method. STEP 5: ROT invites us to compost organic material like yard waste or food scraps. Great for your garden!

  • Grow Your Own Food

    No garden required, make use of your space with some homegrown food!

    A great way to save money, get fit and reduce your carbon footprint is to grow your own fruit and vegetables. If you have a garden, then it is a fun way to make sure your family has access to affordable, healthy, pesticide-free food. Even if you live in an apartment, you can consider using your balcony or windowsills. Failing that, there is a growing movement of community gardens or allotments!

King Oyster Mushroom Pulled Pork

Dinner or Lunch

  • Servings: 3
  • Cooking time: 30 min
  • Full Recipe: Visit liveeatlearn.com
  • 163 Calories

Carbon savings: 85g of mushrooms (0.09kgs CO₂) replaces 170g of pulled pork (2.06kgs CO₂). Saves 1.95kgs CO₂ per serving

By shredding king oyster mushrooms, seasoning with spices, and baking, you can create a vegan mushroom pulled pork that rivals the real stuff!

Ingredients: 4 king oyster mushrooms, 2 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil (30ml), 1 tsp smoked paprika, ¼ tsp salt, ¼ tsp cayenne pepper, 2 cloves garlic minced, ¼ cup BBQ sauce (60g)

Potato and Black Bean Burger

Dinner or Lunch

  • Servings: 8
  • Cooking time: 40 min
  • Full Recipe: Visit liveeatlearn.com
  • 144 Calories

Carbon savings: 56g of black beans with mashed potatoes (0.24kgs CO₂) replaces 170g of ground beef (4kgs CO₂). Savings of 3.8kgs CO₂ per serving

These Mashed Potato Black Bean Burgers make a healthy and filling meal, or you can make them into sliders for a mouth-watering vegetarian option on the table.

Ingredients: 425g Black Beans, 1/2 chopped white onion, 1/2 red pepper, 1/2 jalapeno, 2 garlic cloves, 2 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp cumin, 1 egg, 1 cup panko bread crumbs, 1 cup mashed potato, 1/2 tsp salt & pepper. Serve with hamburger bun and condiments.

Mustard-Glazed Chicken with Arugula and Bok Choy

Dinner or Lunch

  • Servings: 4
  • Cooking time: 40 min
  • Full Recipe: Visit foodandwine.com
  • 144 Calories

Carbon savings: If you want to minimise your carbon footprint without giving up meat, chicken is your best option! Chicken produces 2.33kg of C02 per kg of meat before transport and processing.

Chef Way Takashi Yagihashi glazes chicken legs in a mustard sauce; on the side are buttery bok choy and a generously dressed salad. Healthier Way Opt for chicken breasts instead of legs, cook the bok choy in a minimal amount of oil (no butter) and dress the salad lightly with oil, soy sauce and rice wine vinegar.

Ingredients: 4 skinless chicken breasts, 4 tsp mustard powder, 4 tsp water, 1/2 tsp mirin, 1 1/2 teaspoon low sodium soy sauce, 3/4 tsp sugar, 2 medium heads of bok choy, 1 tbsp rice vinegar, 1 bag baby arugula, salt & pepper to season
